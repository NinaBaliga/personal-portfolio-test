ABOUT ME

NINA BALIGA - User Experience Designer & Digital Strategist

I believe that the best work comes not from the most inspired ideas of one person, but from the collective thinking and examination of a team. Meaningful design is created by understanding people who are different, anticipating what they will want to do, and providing them with the tools they need, exactly when they need them.

I bring over a decade of digital marketing management with a special focus on mobile and web experiences. I'm a civic hacker with a passion for making meaningful change. On a more personal note, I love hiking with my two border collie mixes, maintaining my Yelp Elite status, learning new things (next up Python and Snowboarding), and traveling around the world.

Overview

Marin Bikes, a small company based in the Bay Area of California, was looking for a new mobile app that would help build brand awareness,and bring more customers into their stores to take test rides, that would lead to purchase. (Note: This was a project for a General Assembly class.)

Competitive Analysis

After reviewing the brief, the team began conducting a competitive analysis to see what competitors were doing in the space. Given that the client wanted to increase their customer base, we identified competitors outside of the bike industry to be included in hte overall competitive analysis.

Live Demo
×

User Interviews & Surveys

After conducting the competitive analysis, we conducted several user interviews to provide context for the needs of a new mountain biker or outdoor enthusiast. Building upon the user interviews, we built a user survey that was sent to individuals meeting the target demographic.

Live Demo
×

Task Analysis

In order to identify the key pain points for users in the bike purchasing process, we built a task analysis that identified user action points, considerations and influences. Through this process we identified pain points, and potential functions for a mobile app alleviating some of these concerns.

Live Demo
×

Site Map and Wireframes

To understand how the features would be seen in an overall app, we created an overall structure and user flow for the site, then built wireframes that would incorporate the features identified.

Live Demo
×

Prototyping

After completing the wireframes, we began some basic user testing to see if the structure of the application made sense. After we got feedback and confirmation, we moved forward building the prototype in several different applications, including KeyNote, Axure, and Flint.to. We wanted to find out which of these prototyping tools would be most useful for the user testing.
